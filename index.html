Top Candidates Ranked

Presidential candidates can have a profound impact on the economic state of a country, especially in a nation as influential as the United States. The policies, proposals, and vision set forth by a candidate can shape investor confidence, consumer sentiment, and international trade relations. Often, even the mere anticipation of a particular candidate's election can lead to fluctuations in stock markets, currency valuations, and investment patterns, as stakeholders attempt to predict the economic direction the country might take. Furthermore, a candidate's stance on issues such as taxation, regulation, trade agreements, and fiscal policies can directly influence the health of various sectors, from manufacturing to technology. The perceived reliability and economic competence of a presidential candidate can either bolster or hinder economic growth prospects. In essence, the words and actions of those standing for the presidency can sway not just the immediate economic climate but can set the trajectory for years to come.

Economic Performance

The visualisation provides a detailed depiction of the economic trends in terms of annual GDP growth and unemployment rate from the mid-1960s to 2020. There are evident correlations between significant economic events and the depicted metrics. Around the mid-1970s, there's a noticeable dip in GDP growth coinciding with the "Oil Crisis", suggesting its detrimental impact on economic productivity. Similarly, the "Black Monday" in the late 1980s corresponds with a spike in the unemployment rate. As we move towards the end of the millennium, the "Dot-com Burst" of the early 2000s is marked by a decline in GDP growth and a subsequent rise in unemployment. This trend becomes even more pronounced during the "Great Recession" of the late 2000s, with GDP growth plummeting and unemployment rates soaring. Post this period, there appears to be a steady recovery in GDP growth and a decrease in unemployment until around 2020.
